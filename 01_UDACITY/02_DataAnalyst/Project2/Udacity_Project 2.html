def percentage(column, value1, value2, x1, x2, title, xlabel):
    case1_canceled = df['Showed Up'][(df[column]==value1) & (df['Showed Up']=='No')].value_counts()[0]
    case1_not_canceled = df['Showed Up'][(df[column]==value1) & (df['Showed Up']=='Yes')].value_counts()[0]
    pct_case1 = case1_canceled / case1_not_canceled
    pct_case1 = round(pct_case1, 2)*100
    
    case2_canceled = df['Showed Up'][(df[column]==value2) & (df['Showed Up']=='No')].value_counts()[0]
    case2_not_canceled = df['Showed Up'][(df[column]==value2) & (df['Showed Up']=='Yes')].value_counts()[0]
    pct_case2 = case2_canceled / case2_not_canceled
    pct_case2 = round(pct_case2, 2)*100
    
    plt.figure(figsize=(10,7))
    plt.bar(x=[x1,x2],height=[pct_case1, pct_case2])
    plt.title(title, fontsize = 16)
    plt.ylabel('Percentage', fontsize = 16)
    plt.xlabel(xlabel, fontsize = 16)
    plt.show()

Conclusions¶

  2. 65% of all appointments were arrange by the women
  3. Gender: the percentage of canceled appointments doesn't depend on gender. 25% of all men and women canceled their appointments
